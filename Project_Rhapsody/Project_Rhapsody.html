Problem

The Rock & Roll Hall of Fame has come to RIT's New Media department to look for solutions to various problems that naturally occur in an extensively growing modern museum.

The issue at hand that my team of designers and other developers decided to tackle was a lack of interactivity, personalization, and immersion in the non-traditional museum experience.

Therefore the main problem we sought to our address was that in a museum about self-expression, there was no opportunity for visitors to do so themselves.

Solution

In our discussions, we painted an idea that the modern museum should not only showcase, but entrance visitors to an experience.

An idea of a collaborative, musical makerspace where visitors could cultivate creativity, encourage collaboration, entertain and educate was born.

This is Project Rhapsody, where the visitor becomes the exhibit, and the museum the audience.

Personal Involvement

I personally handled all user interface development as well as the data structure design of the project. The detail-orientated project was refined by both designers and developers.

By developing teamwork skills, I managed to produce designer-intended aesthetic and functionality. My own input adjusted the product to better fit the platform and user experience, as well as learning importaint design concepts from team members along the way.
