Context

BrickHack is a 24-hour marathon to create and develop an idea. Many companies show up to see what interesting projects string up from this friendly contest.

This project idea was completely developed in this timespan, but not developed for use. Due to the fact that our group liked our project proposal so much, we continued to work on this after the event.

This project would count to be extra-curricular, and completely self-driven.

Problem

Within a group of friends, there arn't many quick solutions for finding a right recipe that everyone can enjoy together.

People with allergic restrictions and diets can really drag down everyone's brainstorming and can be even shamed or pressured for having food constraints.

With a group of friends who face this issue every day, we strived to create a web app where restrictions can feel like new possibilities rather than food buzzkills.

Solution

Facebook was decided to be the starting point of the web app, allowing newcomers to use a familiar platform and stay comfortable.

Through Facebook's api, users can select events that they administer to or a part of.

The event administer can then prompt all attendees with an email link to another view of the web app, which lets them fill in anonymous information about their dietary concerns.

Once enough data is collected, the administer can fire up an ajax call to Yumly, and recieve a list of recipies that the group can decide on.
